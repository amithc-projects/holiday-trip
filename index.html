Day 4 — Sunday, Sep 6 🇬🇪
home Base: Tbilisi 🇬🇪

Uplistsikhe Cave Town & Gori Alternatives

Explore the fascinating ancient rock-hewn town of Uplistsikhe, dating back to the early Iron Age. On the return trip, visit Gori. While the controversial Joseph Stalin Museum has exhibits primarily in Russian/Georgian, we highly recommend exploring these excellent historical and scenic alternatives in Gori:

  • Gori Fortress (Gori Tsikhe): Climb this medieval citadel standing on a rocky hill in the center of the city for spectacular 360-degree panoramic views of Gori and the surrounding valleys.
  • Memorial of Georgian War Heroes: Visit the striking circular plaza at the foot of the fortress hill, featuring eight massive, atmospheric bronze warrior statues in mourning/reflection.
  • Ateni Sioni Church: Take a short 15-minute detour south of Gori into the picturesque Ateni Valley (famed for wine-growing) to see this early 7th-century domed church with intricate stone reliefs and historic carvings.
  • Sergi Makalatia Ethnographic Museum: Explore Gori's rich pre-Soviet history through archaeological treasures, traditional costumes, and regional artifacts from the Shida Kartli province.
  • Gorijvari Ridge: Visit the hilltop Gorijvari church for stunning mountain landscapes and the nearby therapeutic thermal sulfur springs at its base.

assignment_turned_in Practical Information & Logistics

gavel UK Visa Requirements

  • Georgia: Visa-free for up to 1 year.
  • Armenia: Visa-free for up to 180 days.
  • Azerbaijan: ASAN e-Visa required. Apply online 1-2 weeks prior. Takes 3 business days, costs ~$26.

directions_bus Armenia Day Excursion

To cross borders between Georgia and Armenia seamlessly, we recommend booking a private guided excursion with an established operator like Gamarjoba Georgia Tours. They supply:

  • Professional driver & crossing assistance.
  • Guided tours of Haghpat & Sanahin.
  • Homemade lunch at a local Armenian estate.

currency_exchange Currencies

  • Georgia: Georgian Lari (GEL)
  • Armenia: Armenian Dram (AMD)
  • Azerbaijan: Azerbaijani Manat (AZN)
  • Tip: Card payments are widespread in Tbilisi and Baku, but cash is essential in Kazbegi and Armenia.
